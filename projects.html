My projects

Here I give you a little review of some of my projects

DesparcheApp

Have you ever been bored because you have nothing to do? We (Ivan García, Mauricio Neira, Daniel Agudelo and me) have the perfect web-app for you! Find interesting plans and places around you! Check out a little presentation here.

Frogger

In this project, Daniel Guatibonza, Andrés Florez and me built the classic game Frogger from the scratch (a full-hardware implementation). I proudly invite you to check out all the documents here.

Deslinealicemos el amor

Have you seen this graffiti before? This piece of art called "El beso de los invisibles" (made by "Vértigo Grafiti") inspired me to write a column about how unpredictable love is. Although "El beso de los invisibles" talks about that people we have forgotten and how love always wins even in poor sectors of population (https://sitiocero.net/2013/09/la-historia-de-un-beso/), I decided to give my inspiration a more general way. If you are interested, download my column here (in Spanish).

A new Snake!

This project is specially interesting because it was my first approach to a microcontroller from the scratch (in a pure hardware way) and I loved it. We (Daniel Guatibonza,Andrés Florez and me) re-built the Snake game using the movements of an accelerometer as the way to change the Snake moves. Visit the repository here.

Libros Escurridizos

This is a great data-viz project about the interesting structure of the Colombian literature environment. Although it was made principally by my dear friend David Narvaez, I made some contributions. The project consists of a specialized search filter, a graph-based data-visualization (that shows the different associations in a dynamic way) and a lot of tears and nights. Look at the repository here.

La cárcel de los sueños

Have you ever imagined that your life is an entire dream? If the answer is Yes, this text is for you. Inspired by a nightmare, I wrote this thinking how dreams can be as real as reality itself. Download it here (in Spanish).
